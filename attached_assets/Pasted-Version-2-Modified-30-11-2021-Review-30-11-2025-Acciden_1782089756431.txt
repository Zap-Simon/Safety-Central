How did this event happen? (Describe the events that led to this incident/near miss occuring
On Tuesday 15th April, our glazier was securing a window at Unit 14 Dover Court as scheduled. The booking was made following an email received from OCHT: “The incident has been reviewed and our H&S team have advised trade can attend Dover Courts but need to apply caution around number 11. Please can you book this job back in, and if any issues arise, please let me know.”
While on site attending to Unit 14, our glazier was approached by someone inquiring about Unit 11, asking if we would be boarding it up. Concerned, the glazier contacted our office, aware of the warning about Unit 11, which was communicated internally through Teams and also noted on the task details for Unit 14, immediately available to the glazier.
Our office advised the glazier not to attend Unit 11

How was this allowed to happen within our company? (what processes were broken, safeguards non-existant, etc that allowed this to happen on our watch? List all that apply)
1.	The glazier did not sign in via Loc8 on arrival at site.
(He was unable to find the assigned WOA job in Loc8) However, he was aware of the warning through multiple internal channels:
•	Microsoft Teams (internal communication),
•	The job card for Unit 14 (which included notes regarding Unit 11),
•	A phone call with our office prior to any action.
2.	Our Health & Safety policy outlines that two glaziers should attend any jobs with warnings, or that a second person must be present on-site. However, our current policy for OCHT was created under the former “A and B category” warning system. OCHT has since changed their alert structure, and our policy has not yet been updated to reflect this.

3.	There is currently no thorough documentation in our Health & Safety policy outlining how site warnings are lifted, or under what conditions attendance is permitted with a active high priority warning in place. This includes the absence of a defined process for verifying clearance with OCHT if a site vist is permitted with an active warning in place.
